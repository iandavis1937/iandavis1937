Problem 1 — The Belly Flop Redemption

Difficulty: Basic mean with a target

Marcus has been training for the Regional Diving Championship for six months — and by "training," we really mean he's been working as a lifeguard at the kiddy pool at the local park. Nonetheless, Marcus has made it to the final round. The panel of 7 judges has spoken, and 6 of them have turned in their scores. Marcus needs to beat his next closest competitor with a mean score of at least 7.5 to advance to the next round. If he can move on, he might be able to get the scholarship that will help him quit his job at the kiddy pool.

Part A

What is the sum of the 6 known scores?

Add all 6 scores: 8.0 + 7.0 + 8.5 + 6.5 + 7.5 + 8.0

Part B

For Marcus to get a mean of exactly 7.5 across all 7 scores, what must the total of all 7 scores equal?

If mean = total ÷ 7 = 7.5, then total = 7.5 × 7

Part C

What is the minimum score Marcus needs from the 7th judge?

Minimum 7th score = required total − sum of 6 known scores

Problem 3 — The Median Twist

Difficulty: Finding the median with a missing value

Last year, a judge at Devon's swim club refused to award his nephew anything other than a 10 (who repeatedly landed flat on his back and was visibly not taking the competition seriously). Since then, the club has switched to a median-based scoring system. In this new system, the panel of 7 gives scores and the median score must be at least 7.0 to qualify. Devon has received 6 scores so far. Her coach, who is hyperventilating with anxiety because she doesn't understand medians, is trying to figure out what score Devon needs from the final judge.

Part A

Sort the 6 known scores from lowest to highest. What is the 3rd value in this sorted list?

Sorted: 5.5, 6.0, 7.0, 7.5, 8.0, 9.0 — what's in position 3?

Part B

With 7 total scores, the median will be the 4th value when all 7 are sorted. If the 7th score is 7.0, what is the full sorted list and what is the median?

Insert 7.0 into the sorted list: 5.5, 6.0, 7.0, 7.0, 7.5, 8.0, 9.0 — the 4th value is the median.

Part C

What is the minimum score the 7th judge can give so the median is at least 7.0? Explain why anything lower fails.

Try inserting 6.5 into the sorted list — what becomes the 4th value? Then try 7.0.

With one judge left — a retiree who struggles to stay awake and gives the same score every time — Priya and her coach are doing frantic math by the poolside.

Part A

What minimum score does Priya need from judge 7 to satisfy the mean condition? (Round to nearest 0.5 if the answer is not exact, rounding up.)

Sum of 6 known = 46.5. Required total = 7.8 × 7 = 54.6. Minimum 7th score = 54.6 − 46.5

Part B

Sort the 6 known scores. For the median to be at least 8.0, the 4th value in the full sorted list of 7 must be ≥ 8.0. What minimum score from judge 7 guarantees this?

Sorted known scores: 6.5, 7.0, 7.5, 8.0, 8.5, 9.0. The current 4th is 8.0. Try inserting a score just below 8.0 — does the 4th value stay ≥ 8.0?

Part C

What single score satisfies BOTH conditions? Is there more than one possibility?

The mean requires ≥ 8.1. The median requires ≥ 8.0. The binding constraint is the mean. Enter the minimum score (rounded to nearest 0.5) that clears both hurdles.

Part A

Using the known final mean of 6.8, calculate the missing 7th score.

Total = 6.8 × 7. Sum of 6 known = 40.5. Missing score = total − 40.5.

Part B

With all 7 scores now known, find the median. (Use your answer from Part A as approximately 7.1.)

Sort all 7: 5.5, 6.0, 6.5, 7.0, 7.1, 7.5, 8.0. The median is the 4th value.

Part C — What-if analysis

Judge 3 (who gave 8.0) later admitted they meant to enter 6.0 — a typo. How would correcting this change the mean? Would Remy have qualified with a mean cutoff of 7.0? How would the median change?

New total = old total − 8.0 + 6.0. New mean = new total ÷ 7. New sorted list: 5.5, 6.0, 6.0, 6.5, 7.0, 7.1, 7.5 — what's the new median?
